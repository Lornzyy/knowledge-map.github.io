ONLINE ADMISSION KNOWLEDGE MAP FOR FIRST YEAR UNDERGRADUATE STUDENT AT THE TECHNICAL UNIVERSITY OF KENYA

A knowledge map describes what knowledge is used in a process and how it flows around the organization.

- IBM GlobalServices - Technique Paper, 2000

A knowledge map enables an organization to describe or have a view of the knowledge present within the organization, whether tacit or explicit, the infrastructure used to store it and lastly, how the information is disseminated.

Davenport and Prusak state that a knowledge map is the visual representation of important information in an organisation which has been located and it involves people, documents and databases. It serves as the organisational memory as organisation continuously evolves and it captures and integrates explicit knowledge within and without the organisation.

According to the who, what and why of knowledge mapping by Wexler. He explains knowledge mapping as a medium of communication in identifying and locating what is important and actionable information in an organisation therefore acting as a roadmap for them for the important information.

Knowledge mapping is also the visualization of a process or procedure to enable an organization to identify its strength and weaknesses, and in the end enable the organization to gain a competitive advantage over its competitors. Where at times it is used as a review material of a procedure.

An example, is when an organization implements a process or procedure they can use a knowledge map to know where they are hitches and implement strategies making it easier to identify any weaknesses.

Below is the procedural knowledge map for first year government sponsored undergraduate students we were able to come up with:



The Technical University of Kenya, is a University based in Kenya. They aim to offer higher education and research in technology which align with its mission of providing technological education and training and to contribute to the advancement of society through research and innovation.

Conventionally, the Technical University of Kenya used to perform physical admission for the first year undergraduate students. However, due to the Corona virus pandemic which required for reduction in physical contact. The school implemented online admission which was made possible by the advancement of technology. This made the process fast and reduced the physical contact of students therefore protecting the incoming, current students and the staff from the pandemic.

How do students perform the online admission?


Government Sponsored students

KUCCPS gets to shortlist the students that are to be admitted into the school. Despite the fact that KUCCPS plays the major role in choosing the students that the school will admit, the institution still has a say in the number of students to be admitted. This is referred to as the declared capacity per class.

In their case, all the institution does is to provide the shortlisted students with admission letters. Once they receive their letters, they can begin their uploading of their documents online. The gap the team identified was, some of the students do not know how to use computers, therefore they may have trouble uploading them. For those that know their way around the websites, they get to do their online application.

Once it is complete, the students receive a notification saying they have completed it. The coordination of the admission process is done by the academic registrar. The students are then admitted by the deans committee and the senate.



The knowldge map describes the online admission of first year undergraduate students at Technical University of Kenya, from the start to up until the student is done and can now attend lectures.The knowledge maps helped us understand the process and we were able to determine that the most prominent challenge was computer illiteracy.

Whereby, the students who do not know their way around computers have a hard time with the admission. To combat this we thought making a knowledge map will easen the process, when a student is stuck they can follow the map and see where they are at and what the next step is.
This will help give them a footing of what they should do and avoid being confused and loathing the process. Additionally, the knowledge map will easen the inquiries since they will have the knowledge map will answer their questions.
